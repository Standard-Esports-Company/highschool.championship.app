The Rules

This set of rules contains the Official Rules relating to the administration of the Queensland High School League of Legends Championship Series and affiliated events.

This set of rules does not contain advice pertaining to technique. Advice on general practice and technique can be found in the Official Handbook.

Copyright in this work vests in the University of Queensland Union. Schools participating in competition conducted by the UQU are authorised to make copies of this publication for the purpose of distribution to those students, staff and parents participating in or assisting with participation in the competitions.

Definitions

  1. Words appearing in the substance of these Rules and appearing in this section have the following meaning:

    Exceptional Circumstances
    Include, but are not limited to a student ceasing to attend a school and the school being permanently or temporarily shut down. Behaviour amounting to an Exceptional Circumstance includes extreme harassment or verbal abuse of a participant or referee.
    Compulsory School Events
    Include, but are not limited to, mandatory school examinations. The meaning of “Compulsory School Events” excludes school and inter-school sporting events and music concerts, regardless of school policy on attendance. “Compulsory School Events” only refers to events taking place at the same time as the scheduled match. It does not include events taking place the morning after the scheduled match.
    Game
    An instance of competition that is played until a winner is determined.
    Match
    A set of Games. The winner of a match is the team that wins the majority of Games within it.
General

  1. Teams participating in the Schools Competition must adhere to the Competition Rules.
  2. The Tournament Organiser in consultation with the University of Queensland Union Esports Department will make the determination of any matter not covered by these rules, including the application of these rules in Exceptional Circumstances.
  3. The Tournament Organiser may credit a team with a win if they consider that the circumstances demand it. This need not affect any other team.
  4. The decision of the Tournament Organiser shall be final. An appeal may only be brought to the Esports Department from a decision which is manifestly unfair.
  5. Any reference in these rules (except for Rules 2.5 and 2.6) to the Tournament Organiser shall be read as meaning:
    1. The UQU Tournament Organiser (High Schools Program) in person
    2. The UQU Esports Representative (High Schools Program) in person
    3. A Delegate, as designated by the Tournament Organiser or the UQU Esports Department from time to time.
  6. The Tournament Organiser may reverse any determination or decision made by a Delegate.
     Correspondence

  1. All formal communications issued by the Tournament Organiser will be made or confirmed in writing. Persons following directives not confirmed in writing do so at their own risk and any consequent breach of any rule specified below will be treated accordingly.
  2. All formal communications to UQU must be made or confirmed in writing. Where these rules require notification of the Tournament Organiser of any matter, correspondence relating to such a matter must be forwarded to the Tournament Organiser within seven days of the matter arising.
  3. Only correspondence forwarded in writing within the time specified in Rule 3.2 will be investigated
  4. Correspondence seeking to invoke the application of any rule specified below must state the following:
    1. The date, and game/tournament code (if applicable) of the match;
    2. The teams competing;
    3. The name or IGN of the referee (if relevant to the application);
    4. The basis of the application;
    5. The name, telephone number or other details of the applicant; and
    6. The proposed resolution to the conflict.
Team Nominations

  1. Team nominations for the Schools Competition will not be accepted unless the School Coach / Coordinator undertakes on behalf of the teams nominated to comply with the Competition Rules by signing the undertaking to this effect provided in the nomination form.
Age Requirements

  1. Students must be at least 13 years of age to participate in the Schools Competition.
  2. The age rules are to apply as at the end of the year for which registration is sought.
  3. If age limit requirements are breached, the team in breach will be deemed ineligible for the Finals Series. Teams having competed against a team in breach of the age limit requirements will be accredited a win.
  4. If requested, the ages of players nominated to participate in the Schools Competition must be completed in a space provided on the nomination form.
Team Responsibilities

  1. It is the responsibility of each team to read the Draw correctly and be in attendance at the relevant game start time.
Team Membership

  1. Teams must contain a minimum of five members. Teams may not contain more than nine members.
  2. The Tournament Organiser may authorise teams of more than nine members at their discretion.
  3. No player may compete more than once in the same round.
  4. Variation of team membership
    1. Team membership may be varied for matches occurring on the first and second preliminary rounds. For example, a player allocated to a different team for the same school, [or an unregistered player], may represent that team in the Round 2 match in the place of a player who represented that team in Round 1.
    2. Team membership as of the commencement of that team’s Round 3 match must consist only of players who had previously represented that team in either Round 1 or Round 2, including as a substitute. For the avoidance of doubt, a team cannot introduce a member in Round 3 who was not a part of that team in either Round 1 or Round 2, but its membership can be constituted of any combination of players who represented that team in either Round 1 or Round 2.
    3. Final team membership must be confirmed with the UQU before the commencement of that team’s Round 3 match. Once confirmed, team membership is fixed and may not be changed.
    4. Teams changing members in breach of (c) will be deemed to have forfeited the match. The team competing against the team in breach will be accredited a win.
    5. If a team is requested by the UQU to confirm final team membership, and fails to do so before the commencement of that team’s Round 3 match, that team may be deemed ineligible for the Finals Series pending confirmation of their membership.
     Substitution of Team Members

  1. Substitute players may be:
    1. Registered players from that teams school; or
    2. Unregistered players that are not students of any school with a registered team.
  2. Substitute players not meeting any of these qualifications may not be used.
  3. Where this rule is breached, the team in breach will be deemed to have forfeited the match. The team competing against a team in breach of this rule will be accredited a win.
  4. Where a team believes this rule has been breached, a representative of that team must notify the Tournament Organiser in writing of its belief.
  5. The Tournament Organiser will determine whether or not a team is held to have breached this rule. This specifically includes determining whether given circumstances constitute a legitimate requirement for a substitute. Any determination may be made prior to the match if the Tournament Organiser considers this appropriate.
  6. Substitutions may not take place after a game has started. If a player becomes unable to act after that time, the team must conclude that game without that player. For the avoidance of doubt, a player may substitute between games within the same match.
     Replacement of Team Members

  1. After the second round, a team member may only be replaced with the approval of the Tournament Organiser and only if there are Exceptional Circumstances.

    NB: This rule relates to the permanent replacement of a player as a member of a team. It does not relate to the substitution of a player for reasons of temporary unavailability to play.

     Postponements

  1. Applications for postponements must be made to the Tournament Organiser.
  2. Applications for postponements should be made as soon as possible and must be made a minimum of one week in advance of the date scheduled for the match. Applications made for postponements after this deadline will be refused.
  3. The Tournament Organiser may only grant a postponement if the team not applying to postpone the match agrees to postpone the match.
  4. The approval of applications for postponements is at the discretion of the Tournament Organiser.
  5. Where an application for a postponement is granted, the teams participating in the postponed match must, in consultation with the Tournament Organiser, determine a time for the postponed match. Schools must act reasonably when approached to make arrangements for the postponement of a match.
  6. Where an application for a postponement is granted but the team against whom the postponement is sought is unable to compete at a later time, the team having been granted a postponement will be deemed to have lost the match. Forfeit rules will not apply.
  7. Applications for postponements for live finals matches will not be granted.
     Forfeits

  1. Teams must ordinarily seek a postponement.
  2. Teams forfeiting a match or deemed to have forfeited a match will be ineligible for the Finals Series.
  3. The Tournament Organiser may only grant a postponement if the team not applying to postpone the match agrees to postpone the match.
  4. A forfeiting team may apply to the Tournament Organiser to have Rules 11.2 waived in Exceptional Circumstances.
  5. Teams or schools forfeiting on a regular basis whether with or without notice will incur a warning. Failure to act in accordance with this warning will be penalised at the discretion of the Tournament Organiser. Penalties may include, but are not limited to, excluding the school from registering teams in the competition in the following year.
     Officiating

  1. Referees are given responsibility for making judgements on match-related issues immediately before, during and after a match is played. These responsibilities include but are not limited to:
    1. Checking the team’s lineup prior to a match,
    2. Monitoring cheating or unsportsmanlike behaviour in game,
    3. Ordering pause/resume during play,
    4. Issuing penalties in response to Rule violations, including during the match,
    5. Confirming the end of the match and its results.
  2. A representative of a team dissatisfied with a referee’s decision may request the name of the referee and seek further justification of the decision given.
  3. The abuse of referees will not be tolerated.
    1. Abuse of a referee will be penalised at the discretion of the Tournament Organiser.
    2. An initial instance of abuse will ordinarily incur only a warning. However, an initial instance of abuse may incur a more serious penalty in Extraordinary Circumstances. This will be determined at the discretion of the Tournament Organiser.
  4. Complaints regarding officiating must be forwarded to the Tournament Organiser and meet the following requirements:
    1. Be sent from the school’s coach or coordinator;
    2. Be in writing; and
    3. Be submitted within seven days of the making of the decision to which the complaint relates.
  5. Complaints must state the following:
    1. The date, and game/tournament code (if applicable) of the game or match;
    2. The teams competing;
    3. The name of the referee;
    4. The rules-informed basis of the complaint; and
    5. The name and email of the complainant.
  6. Only complaints in compliance with Rules 12.3 and 12.4 will be investigated. All other complaints will be returned, unanswered, to the complainant for rectification.
  7. Discretion to review a referee’s decision lies with the Tournament Organiser.
  8. Complaints lacking sufficient detail may be rejected at the discretion of the Tournament Organiser.
  9. Teams or schools regularly submitting complaints that are not upheld will incur a warning. Further submission of unsubstantiated complaints will be dealt with at the Tournament Organiser’s discretion. This may involve a decision to cease investigating complaints made by that team or school.
     Game Rules

  1. Unless agreed to by the Tournament Organiser prior to the match beginning, all games shall be drafted using a Tournament Draft method.
  2. Referees may choose to employ the in game Tournament Draft mode feature, or use a manual draft through the in-game chat, or any other method they see fit at their own discretion.
  3. The UQU Esports Department may approve restrictions on gameplay if there are known bugs in the game, if allowing certain gameplay elements would defeat the aims and objectives of the program including sportsmanship, or for any other reason they determine.
  4. Prior to the Final Series teams shall be assigned Red-Side and Blue-Side at random for the first game, and will alternate sides every game after for the remainder of the match.
  5. During the Final Series the top seeded team will choose their preferred side for the first game, and will alternate sides every game after for the remainder of the match.
     Result Reports

  1. Schools are required to submit Result Reports in accordance with directions given by the Tournament Organiser.
  2. Teams failing to comply with Rule 13.1 may be deemed ineligible for the Finals Series.
     Misreading the Draw

  1. It is the duty of teams to correctly interpret the draw.
  2. Where changes have been made to the draw:
    1. The school’s coach / coordinator will be notified by the Tournament Organiser,
    2. The changed draw, as notified, will be the correct draw and will override any other draw.
      NB: This includes the draw on the tournament website.
    3. Once a notification has been validly sent to a school’s coach / coordinator, that notification is deemed to have been received by the coach / coordinator.
    4. It is the duty of coaches / coordinators to inform all relevant parties of the change to the draw, including coaches, players and parents. The failure of a coach / coordinator to inform relevant parties of the change to the draw will not exempt a team from the application of Rules 15.3 or 15.4.
  3. Where a team is not present within 15 minutes of the commencement of the match, the team present will be accredited a win. The team not present will be deemed to have forfeited the match without notice.
  4. Where there is disagreement as to whether or not a team has misread the Draw the teams in disagreement should contact the Tournament Organiser. In such a situation the determination of the Tournament Organiser as to the correct reading of the Draw is final.
     Language

  1. Players must not use bad language.
  2. Breaches of this rule will be penalised at the discretion of the Tournament Organiser.
  3. Where a team believes that this rule has been breached, a representative of that team must notify the Tournament Organiser in writing of its belief.
  4. The Tournament Organiser will determine whether or not a team is held to have breached this rule.
     Unsportsmanlike Behaviour

  1. Players must behave in an appropriate manner in game and out of game.
  2. Teams are expected to play at their best at all times within any game, and to avoid any behaviour inconsistent with good sportsmanship, honesty or fair play.
  3. The following conduct is prohibited and may result in disqualification of individual players, teams or schools at the discretion of the Tournament Organiser:
    1. Collusion among teams, or among players from different teams,
    2. Modifying or hacking the game client by any player, or person acting on behalf of a player,
    3. Intentionally exploiting a game bug to seek an unfair advantage,
    4. Looking at, or attempting to look at another team through a spectator client, online broadcast, or any other medium,
    5. Playing using another person’s account, or attempting to play using another person’s account,
    6. Intentionally disconnecting without an explicitly-stated, valid reason.
     Final Series

  1. The Tournament Organiser shall determine teams to be invited to take part in the finals series of the Queensland Schools' Competition.
  2. The Tournament Organiser may have regard to results, breaches of these rules, postponements, forfeits, outstanding monies owing, other complaints or any other matter in determining if a team may be invited to take part in the finals.
  3. The Tournament Organiser may make any such conditions as they see fit to Schools or teams taking part in the Final Series.
  4. There will be no postponements in the finals series.
