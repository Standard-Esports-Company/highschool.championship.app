The Rules

This set of rules contains the Official Rules relating to the administration of the Queensland High School League of Legends Championship Series and affiliated events.

This set of rules does not contain advice pertaining to technique. Advice on general practice and technique can be found in the Official Handbook.

1.      Definitions

1.1      Words appearing in the substance of these Rules and appearing in this section have the following meaning:

  • Exceptional Circumstances

    • Include, but are not limited to a student ceasing to attend a school and the school being permanently or temporarily shut down.

    Behaviour amounting to an Exceptional Circumstance includes extreme harassment or verbal abuse of a participant or referee.

  • Compulsory School Events

    • Include, but are not limited to, mandatory school examinations. The meaning of “Compulsory School Events” excludes school and inter-school sporting events and music concerts, regardless of school policy on attendance. “Compulsory School Events” only refers to events taking place at the same time as the scheduled match. It does not include events taking place the morning after the scheduled match.

  • Game

    • An instance of competition that is played until a winner is determined.

  • Match

    • A set of Games. The winner of a match is the team that wins the majority of Games within it.

2.      General

2.1.      Teams participating in the Schools Competition must adhere to the Competition Rules.
2.2.      The Tournament Organiser in consultation with the University of Queensland Union Esports Department will make the determination of any matter not covered by these rules, including the application of these rules in Exceptional Circumstances.
2.3.      The Tournament Organiser may credit a team with a win if they consider that the circumstances demand it. This need not affect any other team.
2.4.      The decision of the Tournament Organiser shall be final. An appeal may only be brought to the Esports Department from a decision which is manifestly unfair.
2.5.      Any reference in these rules (except for Rules 2.5 and 2.6) to the Tournament Organiser shall be read as meaning:
     (a)      The UQU Tournament Organiser (High Schools Program) in person;
     (b)      The UQU Esports Representative (High Schools Program) in person;
     (c)      A Delegate, as designated by the Tournament Organiser or the UQU Esports Department from time to time.
2.6.      The Tournament Organiser may reverse any determination or decision made by a Delegate.
