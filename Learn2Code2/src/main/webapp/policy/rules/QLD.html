The Rules

This set of rules contains the Official Rules relating to the administration of the Queensland High School League of Legends Championship Series and affiliated events.

This set of rules does not contain advice pertaining to technique. Advice on general practice and technique can be found in the Official Handbook.

Copyright in this work vests in the University of Queensland Union. Schools participating in competition conducted by the UQU are authorised to make copies of this publication for the purpose of distribution to those students, staff and parents participating in or assisting with participation in the competitions.

1.      Definitions

1.1 Words appearing in the substance of these Rules and appearing in this section have the following meaning:

Exceptional Circumstances
Include, but are not limited to a student ceasing to attend a school and the school being permanently or temporarily shut down.
Behaviour amounting to an Exceptional Circumstance includes extreme harassment or verbal abuse of a participant or referee.
Compulsory School Events
Include, but are not limited to, mandatory school examinations. The meaning of “Compulsory School Events” excludes school and inter-school sporting events and music concerts, regardless of school policy on attendance. “Compulsory School Events” only refers to events taking place at the same time as the scheduled match. It does not include events taking place the morning after the scheduled match.
Game
An instance of competition that is played until a winner is determined.
Match
A set of Games. The winner of a match is the team that wins the majority of Games within it.
2.      General

2.1. Teams participating in the Schools Competition must adhere to the Competition Rules.
2.2. The Tournament Organiser in consultation with the University of Queensland Union Esports Department will make the determination of any matter not covered by these rules, including the application of these rules in Exceptional Circumstances.
2.3.      The Tournament Organiser may credit a team with a win if they consider that the circumstances demand it. This need not affect any other team.
2.4.      The decision of the Tournament Organiser shall be final. An appeal may only be brought to the Esports Department from a decision which is manifestly unfair.
2.5.      Any reference in these rules (except for Rules 2.5 and 2.6) to the Tournament Organiser shall be read as meaning:

  1.      The UQU Tournament Organiser (High Schools Program) in person;
  2.      The UQU Esports Representative (High Schools Program) in person;
  3.      A Delegate, as designated by the Tournament Organiser or the UQU Esports Department from time to time.

2.6.      The Tournament Organiser may reverse any determination or decision made by a Delegate.

3.      Correspondence

3.1.      All formal communications issued by the Tournament Organiser will be made or confirmed in writing. Persons following directives not confirmed in writing do so at their own risk and any consequent breach of any rule specified below will be treated accordingly.
3.2.      All formal communications to UQU must be made or confirmed in writing. Where these rules require notification of the Tournament Organiser of any matter, correspondence relating to such a matter must be forwarded to the Tournament Organiser within seven days of the matter arising.
3.3.      Only correspondence forwarded in writing within the time specified in Rule 3.2 will be investigated
3.4.      Correspondence seeking to invoke the application of any rule specified below must state the following:

  1.      The date, and game/tournament code (if applicable) of the match;
  2.      The teams competing;
  3.      The name or IGN of the referee (if relevant to the application);
  4.      The basis of the application;
  5.      The name, telephone number or other details of the applicant; and
  6.      The proposed resolution to the conflict.
4.      Team Nominations

4.1.      Team nominations for the Schools Competition will not be accepted unless the School Coach / Coordinator undertakes on behalf of the teams nominated to comply with the Competition Rules by signing the undertaking to this effect provided in the nomination form.

5.      Age Requirements

5.1.      Students must be at least 13 years of age to participate in the Schools Competition.
5.2.      The age rules are to apply as at the end of the year for which registration is sought.
5.3.      If age limit requirements are breached, the team in breach will be deemed ineligible for the Finals Series. Teams having competed against a team in breach of the age limit requirements will be accredited a win.
5.4.      If requested, the ages of players nominated to participate in the Schools Competition must be completed in a space provided on the nomination form.

6.      Team Responsibilities

6.1.      It is the responsibility of each team to read the Draw correctly and be in attendance at the relevant game start time.

7.      Changes to our Privacy Policy

7.1.      Teams must contain a minimum of five members. Teams may not contain more than nine members.
7.2.      The Tournament Organiser may authorise teams of more than nine members at their discretion.
7.3.      No player may compete more than once in the same round.
7.4.      Variation of team membership

  1.      Team membership may be varied for matches occurring on the first and second preliminary rounds. For example, a player allocated to a different team for the same school, [or an unregistered player], may represent that team in the Round 2 match in the place of a player who represented that team in Round 1.
  2.      Team membership as of the commencement of that team’s Round 3 match must consist only of players who had previously represented that team in either Round 1 or Round 2, including as a substitute. For the avoidance of doubt, a team cannot introduce a member in Round 3 who was not a part of that team in either Round 1 or Round 2, but its membership can be constituted of any combination of players who represented that team in either Round 1 or Round 2.
  3.      Final team membership must be confirmed with the UQU before the commencement of that team’s Round 3 match. Once confirmed, team membership is fixed and may not be changed.
  4.      Teams changing members in breach of (c) will be deemed to have forfeited the match. The team competing against the team in breach will be accredited a win.
  5.      If a team is requested by the UQU to confirm final team membership, and fails to do so before the commencement of that team’s Round 3 match, that team may be deemed ineligible for the Finals Series pending confirmation of their membership.
